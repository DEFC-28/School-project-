Background Information

Exact Location of India :

India is located in South Asia, bordered by countries like Pakistan, Nepal, China, and Bangladesh. It lies between 8° and 37° North latitude and 68° and 97° East longitude. The country stretches from the Himalayan mountains in the north to the Indian Ocean in the south.

Biodiversity :

India's biodiversity is rich and varied, including ecosystems such as tropical rainforests, deciduous forests, mangroves, grasslands, and deserts. The Himalayas in the north provide unique alpine biodiversity, while the Western Ghats and Sundarbans are known for their endemic species. The country is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including Bengal tigers, Asian elephants, and numerous species of birds, reptiles, and insects.

In terms of demographics and social aspects, India is one of the most densely populated countries in the world. Many rural communities depend heavily on agriculture, which in turn relies on monsoon rainfall. The monsoons directly influence food production, water availability, health conditions, and the overall economic stability of millions of people. Therefore, the summer monsoons have both beneficial and disruptive impacts on India’s social and environmental systems.
